04.The ad angle

What we open with — and how we land on the version that actually works.

We lead with: "lock in your electric rate"

Texas power prices keep climbing as data-center demand grows. So the message is simple: instead of renting power from a utility that keeps raising rates, you lock in a flat rate with solar — paired with the offer, solar from $167/mo and up to 30% back on a home battery.

That's the theme. From there we run a handful of angles to see which one gets the most response:

  • Rising bills. "Texas electric bills keep climbing — you can opt out."
  • Ownership. "Why keep renting your power? Own it, and lock your rate for good."
  • Neighbors. "Your neighbors are locking in today's rate before the next hike."
  • Calculator-led. "See exactly what you'd save in 60 seconds — no pushy sales call."

How we find the angle that works

We don't bet everything on one ad. We run several angles at once, let the results decide, and put out fresh ads every week until we land on what works.

Launch
Run several angles side by sideEach gets its own image and headline, so we're testing real alternatives — not guessing.
Measure
Watch the numbers for a weekCost per lead, click-through, and how many people actually finish the calculator.
Cut & scale
Drop what's flat, fund what's workingBudget moves to the angles earning their keep.
Every week
Put out new ads and keep refiningFresh ads each week until we find the winner — then we put more budget behind it.
