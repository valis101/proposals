Three things this says

  • You've more than doubled in two seasons — 172 leads, then 206, then 370.
  • It's getting more concentrated, not less. October and November were 67% of your season in 2023. Last year they were 77%.
  • The season now starts in August. You had zero August leads in 2023, one in 2024, and 25 last year. Waiting until October to be ready is a season behind.

What November actually looks like

  • 177 leads in one month last November — about six new conversations every single day.
  • While you're installing five to six homes a day.
  • That's the whole problem in one line. Nothing about your process is broken. There are just more conversations arriving than one person can hold.
One thing we can't explain from the data. December collapsed last season — 54 leads in Dec 2023, 32 in Dec 2024, then just 8 in Dec 2025. Did you turn ads off, get fully booked, or stop taking December work? Worth knowing, because if those leads are still out there it's a month you're leaving alone.

The mismatch is the finding. Your customers reply in five minutes. You reply in ninety-three. That gap isn't a work-ethic problem — it's that four in ten of them are texting at 8pm or on a Saturday, which is exactly when a one-man crew can't pick up.

Every message that asks a real question lands around 68%. The one that asks nothing lands at 6.3%.

Your opener is doing extraordinary work — 77% of people answer it. For comparison, a good cold text campaign runs 15–25%. Asking them to picture their house instead of pitching them is why it works, and we're keeping it.
Follow-up 3 is dead weight. "Hope you're having a great day, no rush" gets 6.3% because it asks nothing — there's nothing to reply to. Swapping it for a real question should get it into the same range as your other follow-ups. That's a fix worth making whether or not the assistant ever goes live.

06.What we need from you

We pulled the answers out of your own texts, so most of this is just confirming what's still true.

  1. Per-foot pricing. We have $5.50/ft for a first install with new commercial-grade material, and $3.50/ft to hang material a customer already owns. Still right for 2026?
  2. Next-year reinstall. You've told customers "40% off," but your actual quotes ranged from about 35% to 55% off. What's the rule you want quoted?
  3. Deposit. We have deposits only on jobs $3,000 and up, otherwise paid in full on completion. Still right?
  4. Service area. You never put a boundary in writing in three years. How far from Cypress will you go?
  5. Commercial and HOA. About 3% of your leads are businesses or subdivisions. Same pricing, or should those come straight to you?
  6. Permanent lighting. Customers have asked for year-round adjustable lights. Do you offer it, or should the assistant say no?
  7. Mockup turnaround. In peak season, how fast can you realistically get one out — and should the assistant promise a timeframe or stay vague?
  8. Anything it must never say. Install dates, discounts, competitors — tell us and we'll wall it off.
Plus one practical thing: when a customer is ready, how do you want to be pinged — text, email, or both? And to which number?
